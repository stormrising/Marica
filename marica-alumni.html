What Marica Alumni Become

Engineers. Doctors. Teachers.
Founders. Public servants.
People of substance.

Thirty-five years of graduates, 25 students at a time. Marica has never been a large school — which means every one of these paths began with a teacher who knew the student personally.

⚙️
Engineering & Technology

Software engineers, developers, and IT professionals — in Hyderabad's technology corridor and abroad. Among them: alumni with learning differences who studied alongside their peers at Marica and now build technology for the low-vision. That is what inclusion produces.

🩺
Medicine & Healthcare

Doctors, dentists, pharmacists, and healthcare professionals serving communities across Telangana and beyond — carrying into their practice the attentiveness they were once shown as students.

📚
Education

Teachers and educators — including some who returned to teach at Marica itself. There is no stronger endorsement of a school than a student who comes back to stand on the other side of the classroom.

🏛️
Government & Public Service

Government employees and public servants across departments — steady, responsible careers built on the discipline and integrity the school has always held as core values.

💼
Business & Enterprise

Entrepreneurs and professionals running businesses in Hyderabad and beyond. The confidence to start something of your own begins with a school that taught you to trust your own thinking.

🌍
Around the World

Marica alumni live and work across the Gulf, Europe, North America, and Asia. Wherever they are, the same report follows them: they communicate well, they carry themselves with ease, and they get on with people.

The Marica Mark

How you recognise
a Marica alum.

Ask anyone who has hired, taught, or worked alongside Marica alumni and the same observations come back, generation after generation. These are not accidents. They are the product of a school that was built — deliberately — to produce them.

"Children should be guided rather than forced towards the goals in their minds — so that we may discover the particular touch of genius in each one of them."

Marie Christine de Rochemonteix · Founder

01
They communicate

Clarity when they speak, attention when they listen, ease when they present. Built over years — through Karadi Path language learning, dramatics, elocution, debate, and inter-school competition.

02
They carry a can-do attitude

The founder's defining spirit, passed down through the school's culture: if it is worth doing, find a way. Marica alumni are known for taking on things others hesitate over.

03
They work with everyone

Marica classrooms have always brought together children of different backgrounds and abilities. Alumni leave knowing how to get on with people unlike themselves — a skill no textbook teaches.

04
They were seen — so they see others

Students who were known personally by their teachers grow into adults who pay attention to the people around them. It is the quietest thing Marica teaches, and perhaps the most lasting.

Thirty-Five Years

Four generations
of Marica.

1991–2000
The Founding Years

The first batches — students taught by Marie Christine de Rochemonteix herself, in a school being built around them as they studied. Many of these alumni are now senior professionals and parents themselves. Some send their own children to Marica.

2000–2010
The Growing Years

The school grew in strength and reputation. Alumni from this decade entered engineering, medicine, business, and government service across a rapidly changing Hyderabad — and carried the school's name with them into every field.

2010–2020
The Global Years

Marica alumni increasingly went abroad — the Gulf, Europe, North America. The communication skills the school had always cultivated became their calling card in an international world.

2020–Today
The New Chapter

The generation now graduating learns AI, STEM, and robotics alongside the values the school was founded on. They enter a world their predecessors could not have imagined — with the same foundation beneath them.

The Alumni Network

Come back.
Give forward.

A school this size runs on its community. If Marica shaped you, here are three ways to shape the students sitting in your old classroom today.

🎤
Speak to Students

Come back and tell students what your work actually looks like — engineer, doctor, founder, civil servant. One honest hour from someone who sat in their seats is worth a hundred career brochures.

🤝
Mentor a Student

Guide a Class IX or X student thinking about their next step — stream choices, college, career. You once needed exactly this. Now you can be it for someone else.

🎓
Sponsor a Seat

Marie Christine believed every child had a right to education. The Marica Foundation continues that work — and alumni contributions sponsor seats for children whose families cannot afford fees. Quietly. Confidentially. Exactly as she did it.
